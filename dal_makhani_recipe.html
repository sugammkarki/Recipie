Dal Makhani Recipe

Ingredients:

    1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal) 1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma) 1 large onion, finely chopped 2 tomatoes, pureed 3 tablespoons butter 1 tablespoon oil 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder 1/2 teaspoon garam masala Salt to taste 2 tablespoons cream Fresh cilantro for garnish

Spices:

    1 teaspoon cumin seeds A pinch of asafoetida (hing) 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Rinse the black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans (rajma) in cold water. Soak them overnight or for at least 6-8 hours.
  2. In a pressure cooker or a large pot, add the soaked lentils and kidney beans along with 4 cups of water. Cook until they are tender and fully cooked. Mash them slightly using a ladle.
  3. In a separate pan, heat the butter and oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown.
  5. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook the masala until the tomatoes become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  7. Pour the cooked lentils and kidney beans into the masala and mix well. Add water to achieve the desired consistency.
  8. Simmer the dal on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend and thicken the dal.
  9. Stir in the cream and let it cook for another 5 minutes. Adjust salt and spices according to taste.
  10. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve the creamy and flavorful Dal Makhani hot with naan, rice, or roti.
